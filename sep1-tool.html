Enter the charted time and a brief description for each clinical event relevant to SEP-1 review. Replace the example entries below with your patient's documented events. Include all findings that may affect Time Zero determination.

SIRS events: temperature, heart rate, respiratory rate, WBC, bands — e.g. Temp 38.9°C, HR 114, WBC 18.2
Organ dysfunction: lactate, creatinine, AKI, AMS, MAP, platelets, bilirubin — e.g. Lactate 3.8, Cr 2.1 (baseline 0.9)
Bundle elements: blood cultures, antibiotics, fluids, repeat lactate — e.g. Blood cx × 2 drawn, Zosyn 3.375g IVPB started
Vasopressors / shock: norepinephrine, vasopressin, dopamine, septic shock — e.g. Levophed started at 0.05 mcg/kg/min

Based on the CMS Specifications Manual for National Hospital Inpatient Quality Measures (v5.17a, discharges 1Q25–4Q25). Always verify against the current version for your abstraction period.
SIRS Criteria (≥2 required)
Temperature
>38.3°C (100.9°F) or <36°C (96.8°F)
Heart Rate
>90 bpm
Respiratory Rate
>20 breaths/min or PaCO₂ <32 mmHg
WBC
>12,000/mm³ or <4,000/mm³ or >10% bands

3-Hour Bundle Elements
Blood Cultures
Drawn before antibiotics; within 3 hrs of Time Zero
IV Antibiotics
Broad spectrum; within 3 hrs of Time Zero

Measured within 3 hrs of Time Zero
30 mL/kg Crystalloid
If hypotension or lactate ≥4; within 3 hrs
Blood cultures must be drawn (not just ordered) before antibiotic administration. Sequence matters — an order alone does not satisfy this element.
6-Hour Bundle Elements
Repeat Lactate
Required if initial lactate ≥2 mmol/L; within 6 hrs
Vasopressors
If MAP <65 after 30 mL/kg; within 6 hrs
Volume/Perfusion Reassessment
Physician/APN/PA note after 30 mL/kg — focused exam or two of: CVP, ScvO₂, echo, passive leg raise, fluid challenge
Reassessment must be documented by a physician, APN, or PA. Nursing documentation alone does not qualify.
Time Zero — Key Rules
Determining Time Zero (SSPT)

Time Zero is the Severe Sepsis Presentation Time — the earliest time at which the final criterion establishing severe sepsis was met. It drives both the 3-hour and 6-hour bundle windows.

Clinical criteria pathUse the time the last of the three criteria (infection, ≥2 SIRS, organ dysfunction) was documented — all three must fall within a 6-hour window of each other.
Physician documentation pathIf a physician/APN/PA explicitly documents "severe sepsis" or "septic shock," use the earliest time of that note — even if clinical criteria aren't fully charted.
Septic shock takes precedenceIf a physician documents septic shock before clinical criteria for severe sepsis are fully met, use the time septic shock was documented as Time Zero.
Pre-hospital / on arrivalIf clinical criteria or physician documentation of severe sepsis is in pre-hospital records or noted as "present on arrival," use the earliest documented ED arrival time.
Notes without a specific timeUse the note's opened/started timestamp. Fallback priority: (1) Provider Patient Care Initiated time, (2) Scribe time, (3) earliest timestamp at the top of the note.
Direct admits (no ED)If severe sepsis is documented in pre-hospital or transfer records, Time Zero is the earliest documented arrival time to the receiving floor or unit — not the time of the physician H&P.
Common Abstraction Pitfalls
Antibiotics before culturesBlood cultures must be drawn BEFORE antibiotics are given. If antibiotics were started first, the blood culture element fails — regardless of how soon cultures were subsequently drawn.
Using order time instead of administration timeBundle elements are timed by when they were completed (drawn, administered, initiated) — not when they were ordered. An order at 0900 with IV start at 0945 counts at 0945.
Missing 6-hour reassessment documentationThe volume/perfusion reassessment must be documented by a physician, APN, or PA with clinical findings. A nursing "tolerated well" note or vital signs flowsheet does not qualify.
Miscounting fluid for 30 mL/kgOnly crystalloid fluids count (NS, LR, PlasmaLyte). Colloids, blood products, and maintenance fluids do not. Calculate based on documented body weight — not estimated weight.
Applying the wrong Time ZeroUsing admit time or triage time when clinical criteria clearly establish a different Time Zero is a common error. Always identify SSPT using spec logic, not the wall clock at presentation.
Ignoring contraindication and directive exclusionsDocumented patient refusal or a comfort/palliative directive within the bundle window can exclude individual elements. Don't mark a case failed without checking these.
